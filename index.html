About

I'm a Test Engineer in Space Operations at Valcor Engineering Corporation, a precision fluid control manufacturer based in Springfield, NJ, serving aerospace, defense, and nuclear industries since 1951.

My work focuses on testing the components that cannot fail — pressure regulators and fluid control valves that go on launch vehicles and planetary missions. I own the full test cycle: procedure development, acceptance testing, qualification testing, data analysis, and reporting.

Mars Descent Regulator Program
Active Mars · 2030 Planetary Descent

Testing a pressure regulation component for the ESA ExoMars Rosalind Franklin Mission — a lander-rover system targeting Mars in 2030 to search for evidence of past and present life. The landing platform must decelerate from 45 m/s at parachute phase end to under 3 m/s at touchdown using retro rockets. The component I am qualifying plays a direct role in that six-minute entry, descent, and landing sequence.
